**Overview:**
This project focuses on analyzing employee attrition using the IBM HR dataset. The goal is to identify key factors that contribute to employee turnover and provide 
meaningful business insights. The analysis is performed using SQL for data exploration and Power BI for dashboard visualization.

**Objectives**
- Understand why employees are leaving the organization
- Identify high-risk groups (department, job role, age, etc.)
- Analyze the impact of salary and work conditions on attrition
- Build an interactive dashboard for decision-making

**Tools & Technologies**
- Excel - Dataset
- SQL – Data analysis and KPI calculation
- Python (Pandas) – Data cleaning and preprocessing
- Power BI – Dashboard creation and visualization

**Key Analysis Performed (SQL)**
The following insights were derived using SQL:
- Total Employees
- Total Attrition Count
- Attrition Rate (%)
- Average Age
- Average Monthly Income
- Attrition by Department
- Attrition by Job Role
- Attrition by Gender
- Attrition by Age Group
- Attrition vs Monthly Income
- Attrition by Salary Group
- Attrition by Job Satisfaction
- Attrition by Work-Life Balance
- Attrition by Years at Company
These KPIs and insights were then visualized in the Power BI dashboard.

**Dashboard Highlights**
The dashboard includes:
- KPI cards (Total Employees, Attrition, Attrition Rate, Avg Age, Avg Income)
- Attrition analysis by department, job role, gender
- Work-related factors like overtime, job satisfaction, work-life balance
- Age group and salary-based attrition insights
- Interactive slicers for department and gender filtering

**Key Insights (Why Attrition is Happening)**
- Employees working overtime have significantly higher attrition
- Low salary groups show higher employee turnover
- Employees with low job satisfaction tend to leave more
- Poor work-life balance is strongly linked with attrition
- Younger employees (18–25) have the highest attrition rate
- Certain job roles and departments** experience more turnover

**Solution & Recommendations**
Based on the analysis:
- Improve work-life balance policies
- Reduce excessive overtime workload
- Provide better salary structure and incentives
- Focus on employee engagement and satisfaction
- Implement retention strategies for young employees
- Monitor high-risk departments and job roles

**Conclusion:**
This project demonstrates how data analysis can help organizations understand employee behavior and reduce attrition. By combining SQL analysis and Power BI 
visualization, meaningful insights were generated to support better HR decision-making.
